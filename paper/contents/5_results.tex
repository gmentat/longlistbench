\subsection{Benchmark coverage}
The largest family, IFTA mileage by vehicle, contributes 17{,}565 target records across 8 PDFs. The remaining core operations families add return schedules, tax summaries, driver/MVR rosters, vehicle schedules, and external loss-run rows. These documents stress high-density extraction where the main failure mode is list completeness across many pages.

The multi-hop cases stress a different failure mode. The claim packets contain only 77 target incidents, but the evidence for each incident is distributed across distant packet sections. The policy packets contain 345 multi-hop policy records across BOP, WC, and CGL packets, while the two production-like BOP declaration PDFs add 1{,}600 policy schedule records. These policy documents make the target schema heterogeneous: a system must recover locations, coverage items, forms, endorsements, class-code rows, exposure items, and premium records rather than one repeated row shape.

\subsection{Baseline status}
The next empirical step is to rerun one-shot and agentic extraction on the current OCR corpus. We expect one-shot prompting to remain a weak lower bound on the largest PDFs, because the task requires preserving thousands of aligned fields. The stronger comparison for future method papers is among agentic, retrieval-loop, and verification-based systems that can inspect the document incrementally and check record counts against the source.
