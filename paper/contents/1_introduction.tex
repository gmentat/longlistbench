Long-list entity extraction recovers hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured documents. It is common in insurance, finance, logistics, and procurement, but remains difficult to measure. A system may extract the first page correctly while dropping middle records, merging neighboring rows, or losing inherited context later in the document.

Established benchmarks such as FUNSD~\cite{jaume2019funsd}, SROIE~\cite{huang2021sroie}, and CORD~\cite{park2019cord} emphasize key-value fields or short forms. DocVQA~\cite{mathew2021docvqa} and MP-DocVQA~\cite{tito2022mpdocvqa} test multi-page reasoning, but not exhaustive recovery of large repeated lists. DocILE~\cite{simsa2023docile} and VRDU~\cite{wang2023vrdu} include richer business documents, yet production extraction also encounters dense exports, repeated headers, OCR artifacts, split records, and fields distributed across distant packet sections.

LongListBench is designed to measure these failures. For each PDF, a system must reconstruct the entire target list under a declared output schema. The current release contains 36 single-PDF instances and 33{,}450 target records. It combines high-density scale tests with cross-section joins, long-range evidence, distractors, and heterogeneous policy records. All names, identifiers, values, and visible prose are synthetic; private documents were used only to study recurring layout and packet structure.

The release makes three contributions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A public long-list corpus with explicit document-family and cross-cutting stressor metadata.
    \item Auditable artifacts for every instance: PDF, HTML source, OCR transcript, JSON ground truth, and per-document metadata.
    \item Strict record and document completeness metrics, secondary field diagnostics, report regeneration, and reference extraction protocols.
\end{itemize}

LongListBench is designed as a benchmark for measuring extraction systems under realistic long-list document conditions. Results should identify the input condition and extraction protocol and report strict completeness by evaluation role, document family, and stressor rather than relying on one partial-credit aggregate.
