Long-list entity extraction, the recovery of hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured documents, is a core requirement for document automation in domains such as insurance, finance, logistics, and procurement. Recent document-understanding models and general-purpose LLMs have improved extraction quality on short forms and narrow schemas, but robust evaluation of long-list scenarios remains limited.

Many established benchmarks focus on key-value extraction or short, form-like documents, such as FUNSD~\cite{jaume2019funsd}, SROIE~\cite{huang2021sroie}, and CORD~\cite{park2019cord}. Document question answering benchmarks such as DocVQA~\cite{mathew2021docvqa} and MP-DocVQA~\cite{tito2022mpdocvqa} highlight the difficulty of reasoning over pages, but they do not require recovering every record in a large repeated list. More recent business-document datasets such as DocILE~\cite{simsa2023docile} and VRDU~\cite{wang2023vrdu} move closer to repeated structured fields, yet long-list extraction in production often has additional stressors: dense spreadsheet exports, repeated headers, small fonts, OCR artifacts, cross-page references, and records whose fields are distributed across distant packet sections.

We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark designed to measure these long-list extraction failures. The current release contains 34 single-PDF instances with 32{,}654 target records. The corpus is inspired by recurring layouts observed in insurance and trucking workflows, but all names, identifiers, values, and visible document content are synthetic. The dataset includes high-density single-list documents, claim packets that require long-range joins, and policy packets where target records span declarations, location schedules, rating pages, forms, material clauses, endorsements, and premium summaries.

\subsection{Background and Motivation}
This work was motivated by production document-automation tasks involving long schedules, loss runs, IFTA filings, driver rosters, vehicle schedules, and commercial insurance policies. In these settings, a single PDF can contain hundreds or thousands of repeated entities. A system may perform well on the first page but silently drop records in the middle, merge neighboring rows, misread OCR tables, or fail to join supporting details that appear many pages away. These errors are hard to diagnose with small form benchmarks because the primary difficulty is not a single field; it is preserving list completeness and field alignment at scale.

\subsection{Research Questions}
This release is organized around four practical questions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item How should long-list extraction be measured when target lists contain hundreds or thousands of records per PDF?
    \item Which document complexities matter for production-like extraction, including dense tables, spreadsheet exports, policy packets, OCR noise, and long-range evidence?
    \item What metadata, schemas, and scoring views are needed so future systems can compare one-shot, agentic, retrieval-loop, and verification-based protocols fairly?
    \item Can a public synthetic corpus approximate the visual structure of sensitive production documents without exposing private data?
\end{itemize}

We address these questions by releasing structured ground truth, rendered HTML/PDF artifacts, OCR transcripts, per-sample metadata, and a reproducible evaluator. We intentionally frame LongListBench as a benchmark dataset for measurement, not as a method paper. Baseline model results should be rerun on the current OCR corpus before being cited as method conclusions.

\subsection{Contributions}
We make the following contributions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A 34-document synthetic benchmark with 32{,}654 target records across 12 production-like document families.
    \item A set of high-density commercial operations documents covering IFTA mileage schedules, IFTA return schedules, tax summaries, driver/MVR rosters, vehicle schedules, and loss runs.
    \item A single-document long-range evidence suite with 3 claim packets and 3 policy packets where records must be assembled from distant sections of the same PDF.
    \item OCR transcripts for every released PDF, plus JSON ground truth, HTML sources, per-document metadata, and a browsable artifact index.
    \item Supporting evaluator scripts for strict loss-run incident scoring, generic record-list scoring, and agentic sandbox extraction protocols.
\end{itemize}
