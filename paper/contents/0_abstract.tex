Current document-extraction benchmarks focus on short, key-value forms, leaving long-list entity extraction---recovering hundreds to thousands of repeated records from tables and mixed layouts---underexplored, though it is common in business documents such as loss runs and itemized bills. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark that pairs JSON ground truth with rendered documents and paired clean and OCR transcripts, making OCR-specific degradation measurable without changing the underlying document. The base suite spans 80 documents across four difficulty tiers (up to 500 incidents each) and injects seven production phenomena (page breaks, multi-row entities, duplicates, large documents, distractor tables, multi-column layouts, and merged cells). We also release an initial cross-document multi-hop extension with 3 packet cases, 15 rendered documents, and 77 target incidents, where the full incident record requires joining fields across loss-run summaries, policy registers, driver rosters, cause-code legends, claimant indexes, and financial ledgers.

LongListBench is intended as a measurement dataset and harness for future extraction systems, not as a solved-method paper. We report sanity baselines to calibrate the task: simple full-context one-shot prompting collapses on long documents, while stronger extraction protocols, including an agentic sandbox regime, recover substantially more of the list. These results motivate evaluating contemporary agentic and retrieval/loop-based systems against controlled layout, OCR, scale, and cross-document complexity regimes.
