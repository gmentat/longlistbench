Document-extraction benchmarks often emphasize short forms, key-value fields, or modest line-item tables. Long-list entity extraction remains under-measured, even though real business documents often require recovering hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured PDFs with complex layouts and imperfect OCR transcripts. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark for measuring long-list extraction at document scale. The current release contains 34 rendered PDFs, 32{,}654 target records, structured JSON ground truth, HTML sources, per-document metadata, OCR transcripts for every PDF, and evaluator scripts for field-level scoring. The corpus covers 12 production-like document families, including IFTA mileage schedules, driver and vehicle schedules, loss runs, long claim packets, and BOP, Workers Compensation, and Commercial General Liability policy packets.

LongListBench is intended as a measurement dataset for future extraction systems, not as a solved-method paper. Its design preserves an explicit complexity taxonomy covering page breaks, multi-row records, duplicate distractors, large documents, multiple tables, multi-column layouts, merged cells, OCR-conditioned transcripts, long-range evidence, and heterogeneous record lists. All visible content is synthetic; private production documents were used only as structural references for layout and packet organization. The released evaluator scripts support OCR-conditioned evaluation, field-level scoring, and agentic extraction protocols that can inspect, parse, and verify long documents programmatically.
