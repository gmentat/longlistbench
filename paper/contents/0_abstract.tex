Document-extraction benchmarks often emphasize short forms, key-value fields, or modest line-item tables. Long-list extraction remains under-measured, even though real business PDFs often require recovering hundreds or thousands of records from noisy OCR, dense tables, repeated headers, and distant supporting sections. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark for measuring document-to-list extraction at scale. The current release contains 36 rendered PDFs, 33{,}450 target records, structured JSON ground truth, HTML sources, per-document metadata, OCR transcripts for every PDF, and evaluator scripts for field-level scoring.

LongListBench organizes documents by the problems they pose: page-spanning rows, multi-row records, duplicate distractors, large outputs, multiple tables, multi-column layouts, merged cells, OCR-conditioned text, repeated keys, cross-section joins, long-range evidence, and heterogeneous output schemas. All visible content is synthetic; private production documents were used only as structural references for layout and packet organization. The task is to recover every target record from a document under the specified schema.
