Document-extraction benchmarks often emphasize short forms, key-value fields, or modest line-item tables. Long-list entity extraction remains under-measured, even though real business documents often require recovering hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured PDFs with complex layouts and imperfect OCR transcripts. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark for measuring long-list extraction at document scale. The current release contains 36 rendered PDFs, 33{,}450 target records, structured JSON ground truth, HTML sources, per-document metadata, OCR transcripts for every PDF, and evaluator scripts for field-level scoring. The corpus covers 13 production-like document families, including IFTA mileage schedules, multisection IFTA return packets, driver and vehicle schedules, loss runs, long claim packets, and BOP, Workers Compensation, and Commercial General Liability policy packets.

LongListBench is designed as a benchmark for measuring extraction systems under realistic long-list document conditions. Its design preserves an explicit complexity taxonomy covering page breaks, multi-row records, duplicate distractors, large documents, multiple tables, multi-column layouts, merged cells, OCR-conditioned transcripts, OCR-layout preservation, repeated keys, cross-section joins, long-range evidence, and heterogeneous record lists. All visible content is synthetic; private production documents were used only as structural references for layout and packet organization. We evaluate end-to-end document-to-list extraction: given a document and target schema, a system must return the complete target list.
