Business PDFs can contain hundreds or thousands of target records, yet document benchmarks usually emphasize short forms or modest tables. We present LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark for complete document-to-list extraction under complex layout and OCR conditions. The release contains 36 PDFs and 33{,}450 target records with JSON ground truth, metadata, and OCR transcripts. We evaluate two coding agents, Codex GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8, on the released OCR transcripts. By field-pair $F_1$, a lenient diagnostic that gives partial credit for each correct field, both agents look nearly done: 98.7\% and 98.6\%. Our primary metrics are stricter. Exact-record recall counts a record only when every normalized field matches the ground truth; the agents recover 89.5\% and 86.9\% of records. Complete-document success requires the entire record list with nothing missing or extra; only 12 and 13 of 36 documents qualify. Failures concentrate on structural challenges, documents whose records must be assembled from multiple sections: exact-record recall falls to 68.9\% and 60.7\% there, versus 99.3\% on scale tests of large uniform tables. Near-perfect field overlap therefore does not imply complete extraction. All visible content is synthetic; private documents informed structure only.
