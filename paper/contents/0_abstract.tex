Business PDFs can contain hundreds or thousands of target records, while most document benchmarks emphasize short forms or modest tables. LongListBench measures complete document-to-list extraction under complex layout and OCR conditions. It contains 36 synthetic PDFs, 33{,}450 target records, JSON ground truth, metadata, and OCR transcripts. We run four repository-isolated coding-agent configurations on the same transcripts. The newest, GPT-5.6-Sol and Claude Fable 5 at xhigh effort, recover 89.9\% and 90.7\% of records exactly, but reproduce only 14 and 15 of 36 complete document lists. On 21 structural-challenge documents, exact-record recall falls to 69.0\% and 71.5\%, versus 99.7\% and 99.8\% on 15 scale tests. Secondary field-pair $F_1$ remains 97.8\% and 98.9\%, showing that high partial overlap can hide missing or incorrect records. Results by problem expose sharp differences: sparse record enrichment reaches 1.9\% for both systems, while long-range claim joins reach 100.0\%. All visible content is synthetic; private documents informed structure only.
