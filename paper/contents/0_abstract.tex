Business PDFs can contain hundreds or thousands of target records, yet document benchmarks usually emphasize short forms or modest tables. We present LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark for complete document-to-list extraction under complex layout and OCR conditions. The release contains 36 PDFs and 33{,}450 targets with JSON ground truth, metadata, and OCR transcripts. We evaluate two coding agents from OCR using strict exact-record recall and complete-document success; field-pair $F_1$ provides secondary partial credit. Codex GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8 recover 89.5\% and 86.9\% of records exactly, but complete only 12 and 13 of 36 documents. Exact-record recall falls to 68.9\% and 60.7\% on structural challenges, compared with 99.3\% on scale controls. This gap shows why near-perfect field overlap does not imply complete extraction. All visible content is synthetic; private documents informed structure only.
