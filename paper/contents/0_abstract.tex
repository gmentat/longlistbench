Document-extraction benchmarks often emphasize short forms, key-value fields, or modest line-item tables. Long-list entity extraction remains under-measured, even though real business documents often require recovering hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured PDFs with layout artifacts and OCR noise. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark with a reproducible evaluation pipeline for measuring long-list extraction under controlled scale, layout, OCR, and long-range cross-page complexity. Each instance pairs structured JSON ground truth with rendered HTML/PDF artifacts, canonical transcripts, and scripts for reproducible OCR transcript generation from the same PDFs. The core suite contains 80 rendered documents across four difficulty tiers, with 6{,}828 incident rows and seven injected stressors: page breaks, multi-row entities, duplicates, large documents, distractor tables, multi-column layouts, and merged cells. We also include an initial 6-document single-PDF multi-hop extension where target records must be assembled from evidence distributed across distant sections of long PDFs.

LongListBench is intended as a measurement dataset with a scoring pipeline for future extraction systems, not as a solved-method paper. We report sanity baselines to calibrate the task. On the synchronized OCR-condition snapshot, a same-model comparison shows full-context one-shot prompting at 29.9\% weighted field-level micro $F_1$, fixed auto-chunking at 85.4\%, and an agentic sandbox regime at 90.6\%. These results suggest that LongListBench can separate scale, layout, OCR, and extraction-protocol effects, and can serve as a basis for evaluating future agentic and retrieval/loop-based systems for long-list extraction.
