Current document-extraction benchmarks focus on short, key-value forms, leaving long-list entity extraction---recovering hundreds to thousands of repeated records from tables and mixed layouts---underexplored, though it is common in business documents such as loss runs and itemized bills. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark that pairs JSON ground truth with rendered documents and paired clean and OCR transcripts, making OCR-specific degradation measurable without changing the underlying document. It spans 80 documents across four difficulty tiers (up to 500 incidents each) and injects seven production phenomena (page breaks, multi-row entities, duplicates, large documents, distractor tables, multi-column layouts, and merged cells).

Evaluating a single model (GPT-5.5) across three extraction regimes isolates the regime from the model. Using corpus-level field-value micro-$F_1$ on the OCR condition, full-context one-shot reaches 29.9\% overall and collapses to 0.0\% on the extreme tier; auto-chunked recovers most of the loss (85.4\%, 84.6\% on extreme); and an agentic regime---where the model is given only the OCR in a code-execution sandbox and parses it with its own tools rather than a fixed chunking policy---is strongest (90.6\%, 89.4\% on extreme). With the model held fixed, the ordering one-shot $<$ auto-chunked $<$ agentic shows that how a long document is fed to the model matters more than the model itself; Gemini 2.5 baselines follow the same pattern.
