Document-extraction benchmarks often emphasize short forms, key-value fields, or modest line-item tables. Long-list entity extraction remains under-measured, even though real business documents often require recovering hundreds or thousands of repeated records from semi-structured PDFs with complex layouts and imperfect OCR transcripts. We introduce LongListBench, a synthetic benchmark and evaluation harness for measuring long-list extraction at document scale. The current release contains 36 rendered PDFs, 33{,}110 target records, structured JSON ground truth, HTML sources, per-document metadata, and OCR transcripts for every PDF. The corpus covers 13 production-like template families, including IFTA mileage schedules, driver and vehicle schedules, loss runs, Businessowners Policy declarations, long claim packets, and BOP, Workers Compensation, and Commercial General Liability policy packets.

LongListBench is intended as a measurement dataset for future extraction systems, not as a solved-method paper. Its design stresses three practical failure modes: high-density repeated records, realistic insurance and fleet-operation layouts, and long-range evidence where one target record must be assembled from distant sections of the same PDF. All visible content is synthetic; private production documents were used only as structural references for layout and packet organization. The released harness supports OCR-conditioned evaluation, field-level scoring, and agentic extraction protocols that can inspect, parse, and verify long documents programmatically.
