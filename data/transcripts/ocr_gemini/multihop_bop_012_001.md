A. Scheduled Premises And Coverage

B. This form applies to Business Income and Extra Expense at Location 001,
   Building 002, subject to the $125,000 limit and $10,000 deductible
   shown in the declarations.

C. Form BPF 04 39 edition 12 23 must be read with the premises schedule
   and the Actual Loss Sustained valuation entry.

D. Prompt notification of any event that may result in a claim is a
   fundamental condition precedent to receiving indemnity under this
   agreement. The policyholder must alert the carrier or its authorized
   representative as soon as practicable after discovering the impairment
   or liability exposure.

E. The notification must include comprehensive details regarding the time,
   place, and circumstances of the event. Furthermore, the policyholder
   must identify any injured parties and potential witnesses, providing
   contact information where available.

F. In the context of property impairment, the policyholder must take
   immediate and reasonable steps to protect the remaining property from
   subsequent harm. The carrier will reimburse reasonable expenses
   incurred for this temporary protection, subject to overall policy
   limits.

G. The policyholder must separate damaged from undamaged property,
   allowing the carrier's representatives to conduct a thorough
   inspection. Detailed inventories of the affected items, including
   quantities, acquisition costs, and estimated replacement values, must
   be prepared.

H. If the event involves suspected criminal activity, such as theft or
   vandalism, the policyholder must promptly notify the appropriate law
   enforcement authorities. A copy of the official police report must be
   secured and forwarded to the carrier.

I. The carrier reserves the authority to require the policyholder to
   submit to an examination under oath regarding the circumstances of the
   claim. This examination may be conducted multiple times if reasonably
   necessary to establish the facts.

J. Within the timeframe specified by the carrier, the policyholder must
   submit a signed, sworn proof of loss document. This document formalizes
   the claim and includes specific assertions regarding the ownership of
   the property, the cause of the event, and the total amount requested.

K. In liability scenarios, the policyholder must immediately forward any
   demands, notices, summonses, or legal papers received in connection
   with the event. The carrier requires these documents to evaluate the
   exposure and organize an appropriate defense.

L. The policyholder must cooperate fully with the carrier in the
   investigation, settlement, or defense of any claim or suit. This
   includes attending hearings, assisting in securing evidence, and
   facilitating the attendance of witnesses.

M. No voluntary payments, assumed obligations, or incurred expenses may be
   made by the policyholder without the prior written consent of the
   carrier, except for immediate medical aid at the time of an incident.

                       SUBROGATION AND THIRD-PARTY RECOVERY
================================================================================

LOCATION: 002  BUILDING: 002
COVERAGE: Equipment Breakdown
FORM: BPF 04 62     EDITION: 12 23      LIMIT:       $125,000
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Scheduled Premises And Coverage

B. This form applies to Equipment Breakdown at Location 002, Building 002,
   subject to the $125,000 limit and $10,000 deductible shown in the
   declarations.

C. Form BPF 04 62 edition 12 23 must be read with the premises schedule
   and the Replacement Cost valuation entry.

D. When the carrier provides financial indemnity for a loss, it
   automatically assumes the policyholder's rights to pursue recovery from
   any third party responsible for the impairment. This transfer of rights
   is fundamental to the equitable distribution of risk.

E. The policyholder must execute any documents and perform any actions
   necessary to secure and preserve these recovery rights. Any action
   taken by the policyholder that prejudices the carrier's ability to
   pursue subrogation may result in a proportional reduction of the
   indemnity provided.

F. Prior to the occurrence of an event, the policyholder may waive their
   rights of recovery against another party through a formal written
   contract. The carrier acknowledges such waivers provided they are
   executed before the loss and documented appropriately.

G. After an event has occurred, the policyholder may not waive recovery
   rights against any party without the explicit written authorization of
   the carrier. Unauthorized post-loss waivers severely compromise the
   carrier's financial position and violate the terms of this agreement.

H. If the carrier successfully recovers funds from a responsible third
   party, the proceeds are distributed according to a specific hierarchy.
   The carrier first deducts the expenses incurred during the recovery
   effort, including legal fees and investigation costs.

I. Following the deduction of expenses, the policyholder is reimbursed for
   any deductible amounts previously absorbed. The remaining funds are
   then retained by the carrier up to the total amount of indemnity
   disbursed for the claim.

J. Any surplus funds recovered beyond the carrier's disbursements and the
   policyholder's deductible will be remitted to the policyholder. This
   ensures that the recovery process does not result in an unintended
   windfall for the carrier.

K. The carrier retains sole discretion over whether to initiate, continue,
   or abandon subrogation proceedings. The policyholder cannot compel the
   carrier to pursue a third party if the carrier determines the effort is
   not economically viable.

L. In situations where the policyholder initiates their own recovery
   action against a third party, they must notify the carrier immediately.
   The carrier may elect to join the action to protect its financial
   interests.

M. These recovery provisions apply equally to both property impairment
   claims and liability disbursements, ensuring a consistent approach to
   third-party accountability across all coverage parts.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

adjusted settlement rather than from the total scheduled limit.

incident.

are released by the underwriting entity. Deductibles cannot be waived or
absorbed into the final contractor invoices under any circumstances.

If the total assessed damage falls below the $1,000 threshold, the
underwriting entity holds no financial obligation for the event. The
policyholder retains full responsibility for these minor structural repairs.

Gradual deterioration, structural settling, and foundational cracking are
strictly excluded from this protection agreement. These issues are
considered routine maintenance obligations rather than sudden,

policyholder.

Infestations by insects, rodents, or other vermin that compromise the
structural integrity of the building are entirely excluded. Routine pest

A maximum limit of $125,000 is available to address the aggregate

The protection is designed to replicate the net operational profit that
would have been realized had the incident not occurred. It is not
intended to improve the financial standing of the enterprise.

Partial operational suspensions, where the enterprise continues to
function at a reduced capacity, are also addressed. The financial
intervention is proportionally adjusted to reflect the partial continuation
of commercial activities.

ARTICLE II - RESTORATION PERIOD operation

The restoration period commences exactly seventy-two hours after the
destructive incident forces the operational suspension. This initial
waiting timeframe serves as a temporal retention absorbed entirely by
the policyholder.

The timeframe concludes when the scheduled premises should
reasonably be repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with diligent effort.
Unnecessary delays caused by the policyholder or their chosen
contractors will not extend this period.

If the enterprise relocates to a permanent alternate facility, the
restoration period terminates upon the commencement of operations at
the new site. The underwriting entity does not subsidize indefinite
transitional phases.

Expiration of the overarching policy period does not prematurely
terminate an active restoration period. The financial intervention
continues until the reasonable repair timeline is fully exhausted or the
limit is reached.

ARTICLE III - CONTINUING OPERATIONAL EXPENSES

Necessary operating expenses that must continue during the
operational suspension are factored into the financial intervention. This
includes mandatory lease payments, property taxes, and essential utility
maintenance costs.

Ordinary payroll expenses for non-essential personnel are not
automatically included in the continuing expense calculation.
Specialized scheduling is required to maintain the wages of standard
employees during a prolonged closure.

Debt service obligations, including mortgage interest and equipment
loan payments, are recognized as valid continuing expenses. The
principal reduction portion of these payments, however, is excluded
from the calculation.

Expenses that naturally cease during the operational suspension, such
as raw material purchases or outgoing shipping costs, are deducted
from the projected revenue. The settlement focuses strictly on
unavoidable financial obligations.

Extra expense provisions address the necessary costs incurred to
minimize the duration of the operational suspension. These funds are
utilized to expedite repairs or facilitate a temporary relocation of
commercial activities.
