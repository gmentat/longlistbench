                  INCOME INTERRUPTION AND EXPENSE CONDITIONS

This form addresses the financial impact of a suspended operation at
Location 001, Building 002. The suspension must be caused by a
qualifying destructive event that results in tangible structural impairment
to the described premises. The interruption of business activities must
be necessary and unavoidable.

Reimbursement is calculated based on the net income that would have
been earned or incurred, plus continuing normal operating expenses.
This includes payroll expenses, subject to any limitations explicitly
stated in the declarations. The goal is to maintain the business's
financial position as if no incident occurred.

Extra expense protection is included to cover necessary costs incurred
during the period of restoration that would not have been incurred
otherwise. These funds are intended to avoid or minimize the
suspension of business and to continue operations at the described
premises or a temporary location.

The period of restoration begins 72 hours after the time of the initial
incident. This waiting period functions as a time-based deductible for the
business income portion of the claim. Extra expense coverage,
however, begins immediately upon the occurrence of the incident.

The restoration period concludes when the premises should be
repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar quality.
It is not tied to the actual completion date if the insured unnecessarily
delays the reconstruction process. Relocation to a new permanent site
also terminates the period.

**VALUATION IS DETERMINED ON AN ACTUAL LOSS SUSTAINED
BASIS. WE WILL REVIEW HISTORICAL FINANCIAL RECORDS, TAX
RETURNS, AND PROJECTED REVENUE TRENDS TO CALCULATE
THE PRECISE DEFICIT. SPECULATIVE FUTURE CONTRACTS OR
UNPROVEN BUSINESS EXPANSIONS ARE EXCLUDED FROM THE
CALCULATION.**

The maximum available reimbursement for this combined coverage is
$125,000. This limit applies to the total of both business income deficits
and extra expenses incurred during the entire period of restoration. It is
not an annual aggregate, but a per-occurrence cap.

An 80% coinsurance requirement applies to the business income
calculation. The insured must carry a limit equal to at least 80% of the
projected net income and operating expenses for the 12 months
following the inception of the policy. Penalties apply if the limit is
insufficient.

A monetary deductible of $10,000 is also scheduled for this coverage.
This amount is subtracted from the final calculated loss after the 72-
hour waiting period has been applied. The dual application of time and
monetary deductibles requires careful accounting during the claim
process.

The insured is obligated to resume operations, either partially or entirely,
as quickly as possible. Any reduction in the business income loss
achieved through partial resumption of operations will be factored into
the final settlement. Mitigation is a strict requirement of this policy.

We do not provide reimbursement for income lost due to the
suspension, lapse, or cancellation of any license, lease, or contract.
Even if the suspension is a direct result of the qualifying destructive
event, the consequential loss of the contract is excluded.

Detailed financial documentation must be submitted to substantiate the
claim. This includes profit and loss statements, payroll registries, and
utility bills. We retain the right to audit these records and employ
forensic accountants to verify the submitted figures.

                                 EQUIPMENT BREAKDOWN PARAMETERS


This section provides specialized protection for mechanical failure events affecting the machinery at Location 002, Building 002. A breakdown is defined as a sudden and accidental internal failure of the equipment that manifests as physical damage and necessitates immediate repair or replacement.

Covered equipment includes pressure vessels, boilers, electrical distribution panels, and complex mechanical apparatus used in the insured's operations. Standard wear and tear, gradual deterioration, and routine maintenance issues do not qualify as sudden mechanical failure events.

In the event of a qualifying breakdown, we will reimburse the diagnostic costs required to identify the source of the failure. This includes the labor of specialized technicians and the use of testing equipment, provided the failure itself is covered under this form.

Protection is extended to include spoilage of perishable goods resulting from the breakdown of refrigeration or climate control equipment. The spoilage must be a direct consequence of the mechanical failure and not due to external power grid interruptions unless specifically endorsed.

Utility interruption resulting from a breakdown of equipment owned by a public or private utility company is addressed here. The interruption must exceed a specified waiting period, typically 24 hours, before the business income implications are considered for reimbursement.

The valuation for damaged equipment is Replacement Cost. We will fund the installation of new equipment of comparable capacity and efficiency. If technological advancements render exact replacement impossible, we will cover the cost of the modern equivalent.

The Agreed Value provision supersedes standard coinsurance requirements for this specific coverage. As long as the scheduled limit is maintained, no proportional penalties will be applied to the settlement of mechanical failure claims.

A $10,000 deductible applies to each breakdown occurrence. If multiple pieces of equipment fail simultaneously due to a single underlying cause, such as a localized electrical surge within the building, it will be treated as one occurrence subject to a single retention.

The liability cap for this coverage is $125,000. This ceiling applies to the aggregate of all costs associated with a single breakdown, including the equipment repair, diagnostic fees, spoilage, and any resulting business income deficits.

The business income basis for this form includes Ordinary Payroll for 90 Days. This ensures that essential staff can be retained during the downtime caused by the mechanical failure, up to the specified duration.

The insured must maintain detailed maintenance logs and inspection records for all pressure systems and critical machinery. Failure to adhere to manufacturer-recommended service schedules may jeopardize the coverage if the lack of maintenance contributed to the breakdown.

We reserve the right to suspend this coverage immediately if our inspectors determine that any covered equipment is operating in a dangerous condition. Notice of suspension will be provided in writing and coverage will only be reinstated once the hazards are rectified.

             INCOME INTERRUPTION AND EXPENSE CONDITIONS

This form addresses the financial impact of a suspended operation at
Location 001, Building 002. The suspension must be caused by a
qualifying destructive event that results in tangible structural impairment
to the described premises. The interruption of business activities must
be necessary and unavoidable.

Reimbursement is calculated based on the net income that would have
been earned or incurred, plus continuing normal operating expenses.
This includes payroll expenses, subject to any limitations explicitly
stated in the declarations. The goal is to maintain the business's
financial position as if no incident occurred.

Extra expense protection is included to cover necessary costs incurred
during the period of restoration that would not have been incurred
otherwise. These funds are intended to avoid or minimize the
suspension of business and to continue operations at the described
premises or a temporary location.

The period of restoration begins 72 hours after the time of the initial
incident. This waiting period functions as a time-based deductible for the
business income portion of the claim. Extra expense coverage,
however, begins immediately upon the occurrence of the incident.

The restoration period concludes when the premises should be
repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar quality.
It is not tied to the actual completion date if the insured unnecessarily
delays the reconstruction process. Relocation to a new permanent site
also terminates the period.

VALUATION IS DETERMINED ON AN ACTUAL LOSS SUSTAINED
BASIS. WE WILL REVIEW HISTORICAL FINANCIAL RECORDS, TAX
RETURNS, AND PROJECTED REVENUE TRENDS TO CALCULATE
THE PRECISE DEFICIT. SPECULATIVE FUTURE CONTRACTS OR
UNPROVEN BUSINESS EXPANSIONS ARE EXCLUDED FROM THE
CALCULATION.

The maximum available reimbursement for this combined coverage is
$125,000. This limit applies to the total of both business income deficits
and extra expenses incurred during the entire period of restoration. It is
not an annual aggregate, but a per-occurrence cap.

An 80% coinsurance requirement applies to the business income
calculation. The insured must carry a limit equal to at least 80% of the
projected net income and operating expenses for the 12 months
following the inception of the policy. Penalties apply if the limit is
insufficient.

A monetary deductible of $10,000 is also scheduled for this coverage.
This amount is subtracted from the final calculated loss after the 72-
hour waiting period has been applied. The dual application of time and
monetary deductibles requires careful accounting during the claim
process.

The insured is obligated to resume operations, either partially or entirely,
as quickly as possible. Any reduction in the business income loss
achieved through partial resumption of operations will be factored into
the final settlement. Mitigation is a strict requirement of this policy.

We do not provide reimbursement for income lost due to the
suspension, lapse, or cancellation of any license, lease, or contract.
Even if the suspension is a direct result of the qualifying destructive
event, the consequential loss of the contract is excluded.

Detailed financial documentation must be submitted to substantiate the
claim. This includes profit and loss statements, payroll registries, and
utility bills. We retain the right to audit these records and employ
forensic accountants to verify the submitted figures.
