Businessowners Coverage Form - Business Personal Property

Protection under this section targets business personal property situated within the structure at Location 002, Building 001, or located in the open within 100 feet of the premises. The maximum recovery is restricted to the scheduled limit of $75,000.

Settlements are subject to a $5,000 deductible per occurrence. This initial retention is assessed against the total calculated loss before the policy limit is applied, ensuring the insured shares in the initial financial impact of the event.

Valuation for the personal property is determined on a Replacement Cost basis. To qualify for this valuation, the affected items must be repaired or replaced. If the insured opts not to replace the items, the settlement will be calculated using actual cash value.

A coinsurance provision of 90% applies to this specific location. Failure to maintain limits that meet or exceed 90% of the total replacement value of all eligible personal property will result in a proportional reduction of the final settlement.

The classification Contractor - interior finishing under class code 5531 defines the expected nature of the inventory and equipment. Eligible property includes furniture, fixtures, machinery, tools, and stock utilized in the daily operations of the business.

For this classification, stock encompasses raw materials, in-process goods, and finished products awaiting distribution or installation. Packaging materials and supplies used for shipping are also categorized as stock.

Tenant improvements and betterments are treated as business personal property. These are fixtures, alterations, or installations made a part of the building at the expense of Pinecrest Print Cooperative, LLC, which cannot legally be removed upon lease termination.

Leased personal property is incorporated into this section provided the named insured has a contractual obligation to insure it. Such leased items are subject to the same valuation and coinsurance parameters as owned property.

Accounts, bills, currency, food stamps, evidences of debt, money, notes, and securities are explicitly excluded from the definition of business personal property. Separate specialized forms are required to address these financial instruments.

Vehicles or self-propelled machines that are licensed for use on public roads are excluded from this section. However, mobile equipment used exclusively on the described premises may be considered eligible personal property.

Property transferred to a newly acquired premises is protected temporarily for a period not exceeding thirty days. This temporary extension is subject to specific sub-limits detailed in the broader property conditions.

The carrier requires the insured to maintain an accurate, up-to-date inventory of all significant business personal property. In the event of a severe incident, this documentation will be critical in substantiating the magnitude of the loss.

A coinsurance percentage of 80% is indicated for this income protection. The insured must carry a limit that represents at least 80% of the projected net income and operating expenses for the twelve months following the inception date.

A $10,000 deductible applies to this form, which may be expressed as a waiting period or a flat financial retention depending on the specific event characteristics. This deductible must be absorbed by the insured before income replacement commences.

The business income basis is also Actual Loss Sustained, aligning with the valuation method. The period of restoration begins after the initial waiting period and ends when the property should reasonably be repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed.

Extra expense protection covers necessary expenditures incurred during the period of restoration that would not have been incurred if there had been no underlying incident. These expenses must be aimed at avoiding or minimizing the suspension of business.

Costs to relocate operations to a temporary site, or to equip a temporary location to maintain the functions of the Contractor - interior finishing operations (class code 5531), are eligible under the extra expense provision.

Pinecrest Print Cooperative, LLC must resume all or part of its operations as quickly as possible. Failure to mitigate the disruption by utilizing available resources or temporary facilities will result in a reduction of the calculated income settlement.

Continuing expenses include payroll, rent, utility minimums, and debt service that must be paid despite the cessation of normal business activities. The carrier will only recognize expenses that are strictly necessary to maintain the organizational structure.

The period of restoration does not include any increased time required due to the enforcement of ordinances or laws regulating the construction, use, or repair of the property, nor does it include time required to test for or clean up pollutants.

If the insured does not resume operations, or does not resume operations as quickly as possible, the settlement will be based on the length of time it would have reasonably taken to repair the property and resume operations.

Equipment Breakdown Coverage

This endorsement modifies the policy to include mechanical and electrical failure events at Location 002, Building 002. The protection is limited to $125,000 per breakdown, addressing the specific vulnerabilities of the Property management operations under class code 7391.

The valuation for affected equipment is Replacement Cost, ensuring that damaged machinery can be replaced with modern equivalents without a deduction for depreciation. This is subject to the Agreed Value coinsurance provision, waiving standard coinsurance penalties.

A $10,000 deductible applies to each breakdown event. This retention is subtracted from the total approved repair or replacement costs before Cobalt Pine Indemnity issues any disbursement.

A breakdown is defined as a sudden and accidental failure of equipment resulting in physical impairment that requires repair or replacement. This includes electrical arcing, mechanical breakdown, and the bursting or rupturing of pressure vessels.

Eligible equipment encompasses boilers, fired or unfired pressure vessels, refrigeration systems, electrical distribution panels, and mechanical pumps used to service the described premises. Routine wear and tear or gradual deterioration are not considered breakdown events.

The business income basis linked to this equipment breakdown is Ordinary Payroll 90 Days. If a breakdown causes a suspension of operations, the income loss calculation will include the continuation of ordinary payroll for a maximum of ninety days.

Diagnostic costs and expediting expenses are included within the scheduled limit. Expediting expenses cover the reasonable extra costs to make temporary repairs and to expedite the permanent repair or replacement of the damaged equipment.

Spoilage of perishable goods resulting directly from the breakdown of refrigeration or cooling equipment is addressed under this form, provided the equipment is located at the scheduled premises and owned by the named insured.

Utility interruption is also incorporated, extending protection to breakdowns of equipment owned by a utility company that supplies electricity, gas, water, or communication services to the described premises, subject to a designated waiting period.

Pinecrest Print Cooperative, LLC must maintain the covered equipment in accordance with manufacturer recommendations. Failure to perform routine maintenance that directly contributes to a breakdown may jeopardize the availability of this protection.

The carrier reserves the right to immediately suspend equipment breakdown protection if any specific piece of machinery is found to be in a dangerous condition. This suspension will be communicated in writing and remains effective until the hazard is corrected.

Software corruption, computer viruses, and cyber-related incidents that cause equipment to malfunction are explicitly excluded from this mechanical breakdown definition.

An occurrence is defined as all loss caused by, or involving, one or more employees, whether the result of a single act or a series of acts over a period of time. The limit is not multiplied by the number of fraudulent transactions.

Losses whose existence or magnitude can only be proven through an inventory computation or a profit and loss calculation are strictly excluded. The insured must provide independent evidence of the specific theft.

Theft committed by the named insured, partners, or members of the LLC is excluded. This form solely addresses the actions of employees who are compensated directly by the business and subject to its direction and control.

Non-owned auto liability applies to incidents arising out of the use of any non-owned auto in the named insured's business by any person. A non-owned auto is typically a vehicle owned by an employee or partner used on company time.

Ordinary Payroll Limitation

This endorsement restricts the duration of payroll protection within the business income calculation for Location 002, Building 002. The valuation is strictly limited to 90 Days, capping the exposure for the Property management operations (class code 7391).

The $100,000 limit and $10,000 deductible apply to the overall business income calculation, while the 90% coinsurance requirement must be met based on the projected income and the limited payroll duration.

Ordinary payroll is defined as the payroll expenses for all employees of Pinecrest Print Cooperative, LLC, excluding officers, executives, department managers, and employees under specific long-term contracts.

Under this limitation, Cobalt Pine Indemnity will only include ordinary payroll as a continuing operating expense for a maximum of ninety consecutive days following the initial suspension of operations.

The ninety-day period begins immediately after the waiting period expires, regardless of whether the period of restoration extends beyond ninety days. Once the ninety days have elapsed, ordinary payroll is removed from the settlement calculation.

The insured may choose to terminate ordinary employees during a prolonged shutdown to mitigate expenses. If ordinary payroll is not actually paid during the ninety-day window, it will not be included in the Actual Loss Sustained calculation.

Payroll for key personnel, such as the property managers and executive officers, is not subject to this ninety-day limitation. Their compensation is considered a necessary continuing expense for the full duration of the restoration period.

This limitation allows the insured to reduce their overall coinsurance basis, thereby lowering the required business income limit and the associated premium, while still providing a short-term buffer for the general workforce.

If the business resumes partial operations within the ninety-day window, the ordinary payroll calculation will be adjusted to reflect the wages paid for the resumed operations versus the wages paid to idle employees.

The carrier requires detailed payroll records, categorized by employee type, to accurately apply this limitation during a claim evaluation. Misclassification of employees can result in settlement disputes.

This endorsement modifies the Business Income and Extra Expense form and controls over any conflicting definitions of continuing operating expenses found elsewhere in the policy packet.

Ordinary payroll is defined as the payroll expenses for all employees of Pinecrest Print Cooperative, LLC, excluding officers, executives, department managers, and employees under specific long-term contracts.

Under this limitation, Cobalt Pine Indemnity will only include ordinary payroll as a continuing operating expense for a maximum of ninety consecutive days following the initial suspension of operations.

The ninety-day period begins immediately after the waiting period expires, regardless of whether the period of restoration extends beyond ninety days. Once the ninety days have elapsed, ordinary payroll is removed from the settlement calculation.

The insured may choose to terminate ordinary employees during a prolonged shutdown to mitigate expenses. If ordinary payroll is not actually paid during the ninety-day window, it will not be included in the Actual Loss Sustained calculation.

Payroll for key personnel, such as the property managers and executive officers, is not subject to this ninety-day limitation. Their compensation is considered a necessary continuing expense for the full duration of the restoration period.

This limitation allows the insured to reduce their overall coinsurance basis, thereby lowering the required business income limit and the associated premium, while still providing a short-term buffer for the general workforce.

If the business resumes partial operations within the ninety-day window, the ordinary payroll calculation will be adjusted to reflect the wages paid for the resumed operations versus the wages paid to idle employees.

The carrier requires detailed payroll records, categorized by employee type, to accurately apply this limitation during a claim evaluation. Misclassification of employees can result in settlement disputes.
