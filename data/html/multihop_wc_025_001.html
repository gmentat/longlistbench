This policy's premium is subject to an experience rating plan, which adjusts the manual premium based on the Named Insured's past workers compensation loss experience. An experience modification factor, such as 1.04, is applied to the manual premium to reflect this history.

The experience modification factor is developed by the appropriate rating bureau based on reported payroll and loss data from prior policy periods. This factor is applied to all classifications and states covered by this policy, unless a specific state endorsement dictates otherwise.

A factor greater than 1.00 indicates a loss experience worse than average for the industry, resulting in an increased premium. A factor less than 1.00 indicates a better-than-average loss experience, leading to a reduced premium.

The experience modification factor shown on the declarations page is the most current factor available at the time of policy issuance. Any revisions to this factor during the policy period will result in a recalculation of the estimated premium.

The purpose of the experience rating plan is to provide an incentive for employers to implement effective safety programs and manage workplace hazards, thereby reducing the frequency and severity of employee injuries.
