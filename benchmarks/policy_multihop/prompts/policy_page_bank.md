# Policy Page Bank Prompt

Generate reusable synthetic page drafts for one commercial insurance policy.
These drafts will be rendered into HTML/PDF pages and combined with deterministic
declarations and schedules generated by code.

The drafts should look like pages that might appear inside a real policy packet,
but they must be fully original. Use the reference observations below only for
structure and density.

## Reference Observations From Real Policy Packets

- A policy packet is mostly prose, not tables.
- Declarations and schedules are usually short; coverage forms, amendatory
  forms, notices, and audit conditions occupy many pages.
- Real pages often have terse headings, form IDs, edition dates, dense numbered
  clauses, broad references to declarations, and repeated "read this policy"
  style notices.
- Workers compensation packets emphasize information pages, state schedules,
  classification codes, remuneration/payroll, experience modification, audit,
  cancellation, and waiver/recovery endorsements.
- Businessowners packets emphasize described premises, property coverage,
  business income, optional coverages, deductibles, valuation, coinsurance,
  exclusions, and endorsements.
- CGL packets emphasize coverage parts, limits, classifications, territories,
  exposure bases, additional insureds, exclusions, products-completed
  operations, and aggregate limits.

## Style Targets

- Write in a restrained insurance-policy voice.
- Use compact paragraphs that can be laid out in two columns.
- Include administrative clauses, audit conditions, notices, and state
  amendatory pages.
- Make each page self-contained enough to look real but not so specific that it
  conflicts with schedules.
- Avoid copying familiar ISO-like sentences verbatim. Paraphrase policy ideas.
- Avoid standard-form phrases even if they are common in insurance documents.
  Use fresh synthetic wording that describes the same administrative idea.

Generate:

- 5 `policy_form` drafts
- `coverage_form` drafts only when `coverage_form_records` are provided. For
  BOP, generate exactly one `coverage_form` draft for each
  `coverage_form_records` entry and copy the coverage/form/location/building
  metadata into the matching draft.
- 2 `notice` drafts
- 2 `amendatory` drafts
- 2 `billing` drafts
- 4 `endorsement` drafts

Each ordinary draft should contain 4 to 6 paragraphs. Each `coverage_form`
draft should contain 9 to 14 compact clause paragraphs with different headings,
enumeration style, sentence rhythm, and policy mechanics across coverages.
Keep paragraphs factual, commercial-insurance-like, and suitable for a
synthetic public benchmark.

Avoid making the generated forms read like explanatory summaries. Do not reuse
the same title suffix across drafts. Avoid repeated phrases such as "financial
protection", "financial recovery", "financial impact", "this segment", or
"mechanics". Prefer issued-policy language: coverage applies, property is
covered, exclusions apply, limits are scheduled, records must be kept, valuation
is determined, and endorsements control conflicting provisions.
