# BOP Page Guidance

Use this guidance when the line of business is BOP.

Good page types:

- Businessowners coverage form
- Property coverage conditions
- Business income and extra expense conditions
- Optional coverage endorsement
- Described premises notice
- Additional insured or contract status endorsement
- Premium audit/accounting conditions
- State amendatory endorsement

Coverage-form diversity requirements:

- Generate 28 to 36 substantive paragraphs for every `coverage_form` draft,
  organized under at least six coverage-specific headings. Each non-heading
  paragraph should usually contain 25 to 40 words, for roughly 800 to 1,100
  words per form. The rendered form must fill a two-column policy page without
  generic padding.
- Do not reuse a single clause skeleton across coverages.
- For equipment breakdown, write about breakdown events, diagnostic costs,
  utility interruption, spoilage, pressure systems, and covered equipment
  records.
- For business income, write about restoration period, continuing expenses,
  ordinary payroll, waiting periods, and records that substantiate loss of
  income.
- For accounts receivable, write about reconstruction of records, collection
  expense, duplicate invoices, and excluded bookkeeping errors.
- For outdoor property, write about signs, fences, shrubs, debris removal, and
  premises boundaries.
- For employee dishonesty, write about discovery, employee acts, inventory
  shortages, prior knowledge, and separation from ordinary property loss.
- For additional insured or hired/non-owned auto forms, write in liability
  endorsement language rather than property-loss language.
- For exclusions, write restriction and carve-back language rather than limit
  and deductible language.
- Include the scheduled limit, deductible, valuation, or coinsurance facts only
  where they make sense; do not force the same fact sentence into every form.

Synthetic example style:

> The premises schedule identifies the location and building numbers used
> throughout this policy. A coverage shown for one building does not apply to a
> different building unless the declarations or an attached endorsement says so.

> The valuation basis, deductible, limit, and coinsurance entry shown for a
> scheduled coverage are part of the declarations. Those entries do not broaden
> the covered causes of loss or remove exclusions stated elsewhere in the
> policy.

Avoid:

- Excessive tables
- Claim handling examples
- Real carrier form copyright lines
- Verbatim standard-form phrases
